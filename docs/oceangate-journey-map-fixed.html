Pain Points

Risk is not fully visible during early brand discovery.

Non-experts cannot easily judge technical legitimacy.

Trust is shaped more by confidence and exclusivity than evidence.

Legal consent does not always mean informed understanding.

Information is fragmented, uncertain, and emotionally overwhelming.

Credibility collapses because trust was not backed by safety transparency.

Crisis reveals failures in ethics, oversight, and service design.

OceanGate
Response

Promoted the expedition as aspirational and exclusive.

Relied on company-controlled credibility and messaging.

Used confidence, innovation, and founder authority to build trust.

Used waivers and onboarding to transfer risk to participants.

Public communication was limited while media shaped the story.

Prior safety concerns became central to public criticism.

Investigations shifted focus toward accountability and oversight.

Opportunities

Make safety and risk communication as visible as branding.

Use plain-language safety disclosures and independent validation.

Build trust through transparency, not just prestige.

Redesign consent so users truly understand risk.

Use clear, factual, humane crisis communication.

Make accountability and third-party oversight visible.

Build systems around ethical communication, independent review, and safety-first design.
