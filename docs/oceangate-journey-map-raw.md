# OceanGate Trust Journey Map - Slide 4

Use this as the editable raw content for Google Slides.

## Emotional Journey

excited > cautious > reassured > nervous > shocked > distrustful > skeptical

Curve direction:

Discovers OceanGate: high
Researches the Experience: medium-high
Builds Trust: high
Signs On / Prepares: medium-high
Crisis Becomes Public: sharp drop
Trust Collapses: lowest point
Aftermath / Reflection: slight recovery, but still low

## Journey Map

| Stage | Actions | Touchpoints | Emotions | Pain Points | Opportunity |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Discovers OceanGate | Finds OceanGate through media, branding, or word of mouth | Website, promotional content, interviews, media coverage | Curious, impressed, excited | The experience feels aspirational, but risk is not yet fully visible | Make safety and risk communication as visible as branding |
| 2. Researches the Experience | Looks into the expedition, Titanic dive details, company credibility, and cost | Website, articles, videos, company materials | Interested, cautious, uncertain | A non-expert user cannot easily judge technical legitimacy | Use plain language safety disclosures and independent validation |
| 3. Builds Trust | Begins believing OceanGate is credible enough to deliver the experience safely | Brand voice, founder messaging, prestige framing, consultation | Reassured, fascinated, committed | Trust may be shaped more by exclusivity and confidence than by evidence | Design trust through transparency, not just prestige |
| 4. Signs On / Prepares | Signs documents, receives onboarding info, prepares for expedition | Waivers, communication materials, pre-trip support | Excited, nervous, dependent | Legal consent does not necessarily equal informed understanding | Redesign consent so users truly understand risk |
| 5. Crisis Becomes Public | Families, media, and the public follow the breaking story after contact is lost | News alerts, public statements, social media, live coverage | Fear, confusion, shock | Information is fragmented, uncertain, and emotionally overwhelming | Use clear, factual, humane crisis communication |
| 6. Trust Collapses | Public learns more about safety concerns, warnings, and organizational issues | Investigations, expert commentary, reports, press coverage | Anger, disbelief, distrust | The brand's credibility collapses because trust was not backed by safety transparency | Make accountability and third-party oversight visible |
| 7. Aftermath / Reflection | Public and regulators reflect on what failed and what should have changed | Investigations, public discussion, policy conversations | Grief, caution, skepticism | The experience reveals deeper failures in ethics, oversight, and service design | Build systems centered on ethical communication, independent review, and safety-first design |

## Key Opportunities

- clearer risk communication
- independent safety validation
- better informed-consent design
- ethical trust-building
- stronger crisis communication

## Speaker Note

This journey map tracks the customer trust journey around OceanGate rather than only the technical event itself. We mapped how a prospective customer might discover the expedition, build trust in the company, and make a high-stakes decision based on limited ability to verify safety independently. The sharpest breakdown happens when public awareness of the crisis exposes that trust was not built on transparent, understandable risk communication.
